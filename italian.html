Italian Cuisine in São Paulo

About Italian Food and Culture in Brazil:

Italians have been immigrating to Brazil since the 1870s, and since then have been greatly influencing Brazilian culinary habits.The majority of these immigrants had some background in agriculture and/or culinary. Now, there are generations of Brazilian Italians, and beyond that, the regular Brazilian is also enjoying a lasagna for lunch on Sunday's - essential elements of Brazilian culture are all due to the Italian immigrants.

Italian Restaurants:

Bottega Bernacca

Known for its charming ambiance, Bottega Bernacca serves perfectly al dente pasta - some say the bolognese of the best they have ever had. With few options on the menu, the the restaurant is able to offer the best of the best.

Plate Recommendations:

Rigatoni Alla Bolognese, Il Cacio & Pepe con Limone e Bottarga, Carpaccio Cipriani

Piselli

It all started because of a pea, what 'piselli' means in Italian - all the plates in the restaurants are accompanied by one. The plates are inspired by the Piemont region in Italy.

Plate Recommendations:

Ravioli alla Piemontese, Ravioli di fontina alla crema di tartufo negro, suppli al telefono/aranchini

Casimiro

Are you curious to try the infamous Grana Padano? At Casimiro, you will find the best of the best - from seafood to steak, it is incredible.

Plate Recommendations:

Linguine Nel Grana Padano

Tatini

Casimiro is the ‘modern’ version of Tatini. This restaurant is known as one of the best Italian restaurants in Sao Paulo, serving spectacular pasta with great wine selection and great service. Won 2020 Travelers’ Choice

Plate Recommendations:

Spaghetti Firenze dois molhos e dois vinhos, Ossobuco de Vitela com risotto milanêsa
