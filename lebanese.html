Lebanese/Arab Cuisine in São Paulo

About Lebanese Food and Culture in Brazil:

Fleeing from the Ottoman Empire, Christian arabs were tricked by the navigation companies by offering to take them to the US but took them to Brazilian ports saying "It was all America it didn't make a difference". But they rather liked it there and the opportunities the land presented were vast. Hence, now many Brazilians - including myself - have Lebanese, Syrian and other Arab ascendants.

Lebanese Restaurants:

Almanara

Known for its charming ambiance, Bottega Bernacca serves perfectly al dente pasta - some say the bolognese of the best they have ever had. With few options on the menu, the the restaurant is able to offer the best of the best.

*I tend to eat the same things at the different Lebanese restaurants, but each of these have added their own touch to a traditional recipe.
Plate Recommendations:

Esfihas (Carne - Meat, Queijo - Cheese, Zatar), Falafel, Pão Sírio, Homus and Coalhada Seca

Brasserie Victória

Named after Dona Victória who sold her 'quibes and esfihas' to her Brazilian friends that were fascinated by her cuisine. Opening the restaurant in 1947, the restaurant is now a reference in lebanese cuisine and culture in São Paulo.

Amani Restaurante

Just like By Koji (Japanese),Amani is also located inside the soccer stadium. They offer extremely high quality lebanese cuisine, also something you can enjoy during a soccer game. The ambiance is incredible and a great place to go for a date or with family.
